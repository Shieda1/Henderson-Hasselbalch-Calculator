What is the Henderson-Hasselbalch equation?
The Henderson-Hasselbalch equation relates the pH of a chemical solution of a weak acid to the numerical value of the acid dissociation constant ka, of acid and the ratio of concentrations of the acid and it's conjugate base in an equilibrium.
How to calculate Henderson-Hasselbalch equation?
To calculate Henderson-Hasselbalch equation you should use the following formula:
pH = pKa + log10([A^-]/[HA])
where:
pH = acidity of a buffer solution
pKa = negative logarithm of Ka
Ka = acid disassociation constant
[HA] = concentration of an acid
[A^-] = concentration of onjugate base
Why use Henderson-Hasselbalch equation?
The Henderson-Hasselbalch equation is used in order to calculate the amount of acid and conjugate base to be combined for the preparation of a buffer solution having a specific pH.
